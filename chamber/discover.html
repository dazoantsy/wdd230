10 places to visit in Madagascar

The Queen's Palace
Because of its location on one of the highest hills of Tananarive, this palace is without doubt one of the most beautiful tourist sites in Madagascar. Visible for miles, this palace was once a home to the kings and queens of Madagascar. The Manjakamiadana Palace offers an incredible panoramic view of the surroundings and is close to other important historical monuments such as the Catholic Cathedral of Andohalo and the Anglican Cathedral.

Ambositra
In the center of the Big Island is a big peasant village typical of the region. The numerous harnessed zebus that roam the town as needed and its beautiful brick and adobe houses give Ambositra a special charm. There is a soft and restful atmosphere, where the local craftsmen are recognized as masters especially in the field of marquetry and woodcarving. The surrounding villages scattered on the mountains surrounding Ambositra will be an opportunity for travelers to dive into the local culture and discover ways of life very different from those of the urban ones.

Antananarivo (The Town of Thousand)
The sprawling capital of the country spreads its districts over 18 hills. Its galloping urbanization offers a diversity of attractions, suitable to satisfy backpackers in search of a total and sometimes brutal change of scenery, as well as more classic tourists in search of historical monuments and traditional architecture. The latter will enjoy wandering through the Queen's Palace district and its old colonial houses, while the adventurous will meet the locals in the Upper Town by wandering through its winding alleys. Perched on the many high points of the district, they will enjoy the unmissable viewpoints that offer travelers incomparable landscapes.

Diego-Suarez
Located at the northern tip of the island, the bay of Diego-Suarez, which takes its name from the two navigators who explored it in the 15th century, is in fact an assembly of 4 related bays that open onto a narrow channel towards the ocean. Former French garrison and third port of the island, the city of Antsiranana remains strongly marked by colonization and strives to exorcise its old demons by a festive and multicultural identity. The hinterland, unavoidable, is also worth the detour with its typical fishing villages or the national park of the Amber Mountain.

The National Park of Isalo
This immense massif of eroded sandstone of the Jurassic reflects all the diversity of the Malagasy nature. Very popular with travelers, this site is an opportunity to see the famous lemurs in their natural environment. The landscape is the image of the Big Island: wild, multiple and picturesque. The deep canyons dotted with dark caves follow the palm forests that crisscross rivers filled with endemic species. The "Malagasy Colorado" with its breathtaking panoramas and its many crossroads is a trip in itself that will delight trekking and hiking enthusiasts.

The Island of Nosy Be
The island of Nosy Be is considered as the unavoidable pearl of Madagascar. And for good reason, its geographical location in the northwest of the Big Island allows it to enjoy a tropical climate, ideal for lounging on its isolated beaches that slope gently down to the sea. Extreme sports enthusiasts will be delighted by the many diving spots along the coast while more bucolic tourists can enjoy gentle hikes through the sugar cane fields. For travelers tired of their journey, Nosy Be is the resort to see and to recommend!

Tsingy de Bemaraha
200 km north of Mondorava, the Tsingy de Bemaraha National Park is surely one of the most amazing geological sites of Madagascar. Its majestic karst formations that take the form of tapered needles of several tens of meters in height are now listed as World Heritage by UNESCO. The canyons and gorges which navigate between the rocky masses shelter a unique fauna and flora in the world, among which birds of prey, all kinds of endemic plants as well as a good ten species of lemurs!

The Island of Sainte-Marie
On the east coast, Sainte-Marie Island is best known as a refuge and observation point for the inevitable humpback whales that come here to enjoy the calm and protected waters to give birth. Many service providers offer to take you aboard their boats to get as close as possible to the whales without disturbing them. Coral reefs proliferate in the calm waters, attracting many scuba diving enthusiasts and the interior of the island presents an incredible biodiversity and locals always ready to return your smile. A little jewel to see urgently!

The National Park of Marojejy
The Marojejy Park in SAVA, known for the impressive mountain that dominates it, the Montagne d'Ambre National Park in the north of the island as well as the famous Tsimbazaza Park in the capital are also among the sites not to be missed.

The Baobab Path
Only 20 km from Morondava on the road to Belon'i Tsiribihina, a score of majestic baobabs, called "roots of the sky" by the Malagasy, frame a dirt road that winds through the pastures. The place, enjoying the status of a temporary protected area since 2007, attracts more and more tourists who gather at sunset and sunrise to take pictures of these hundred-year-old trees. To get there, Morondava benefits from regular airlines but can also be reached by road or even by boat. A chance for all the amateurs of grandiose landscapes!
